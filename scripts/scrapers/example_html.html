\n\r\n\t\t\t\tNon-Perturbative Phenomena in Particle Physics\t\t\t
\r\n\t\t\tAlthough usually studied only in a perturbative manner, the standard model of particle physics is a full quantum field theory that also allows the description of non-perturbative phenomena. \r\nThese range from unstable but self-sustaining field configurations where the Higgs field is pushed out of its vacuum ground state by the forces of the weak interaction, to the violation of various conservation laws by such configurations. \r\nEven conservation of baryon number, the conservation law that is responsible for the stability of protons and neutrons, and therefore, of virtually all known matter, may be violated by such processes.\r\nThis plays a crucial role in cosmology, because, if baryon number would always be conserved, the universe would have to contain as many baryons as anti-baryons. Various phenomena of this kind, and many of their unusual effects will be treated in this work. \r\n\t\t\t\t\t		\n
