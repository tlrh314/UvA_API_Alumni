API Stargazing night

Always wanted to take a look through a telescope to admire the night sky? Or wanted to ask lots of questions about astronomy? The next APO open night will be on Friday 26 February. This is a night for adults and the language spoken will be English. We’ll start at 7PM with a talk by Dr. Jason Hessels called "Mysterious Radio Flashes from Outer Space", and between 8PM and 10PM there will be stargazing and astro café. In case of cloudy weather, there will be tours of the domes.

Participation in the APO open night is free of charge, but registration is required. Registration starts at noon on Friday 19 February through the form that will be uploaded at aanmeldingsformulier. Please do not try to register by e-mail. The maximum amount of visitors is 80. The stargazing night is organized in cooperation with the amateur society Volkssterrenwacht Amsterdam.

If you would like to stay informed about the stargazing nights please send an e-mail to E.Hanko@uva.nl.

Address: Anton Pannekoek Institute for Astronomy
Science Park 904
1098 XH Amsterdam
You will be welcomed at the reception.

Many paid parking spots are available at the Science Park Amsterdam. The Science Park can easily be reached by public transport: the train and bus 40 stop regularly. The map shows the buildings in the area, including Science Park 904. The observatory can not be reached by disabled visitors. Participating in the observing and the tours is therefore not possible.
