API Stargazing night

Gaze at the stars through our telescopes or participate in a tour through the domes! The latest research is explained in a lecture.

Have you been waiting for the chance to see the starry sky up close? On friday March 28 an English stargazing night takes place at the Anton Pannekoek Institute of Astronomy at the University of Amsterdam. Several telescopes are available to observe the night sky on a clear evening. On a cloudy evening there are tours through the domes and volunteers of the amateur society Volkssterrenwacht Amsterdam will introduce interesting objects to observe in the night sky. De evening starts with a lecture by a professional astronomer and a quiz.

Pre-registration is necessary. Registration will be possible from March 21. No entrance fee. Please bring a copy of your confirmation e-mail. You can show up at the desk in the main entrance hall of Science Park 904 from 18:30h. The activities start at 19:00h. The evening ends at 22:00h. If you would like to stay informed about the stargazing nights please send an e-mail to F.Buurmeijer@uva.nl

Address: Science Park 904
1098 XH Amsterdam

Many paid parking spots are available at the Science Park Amsterdam. The Science Park can easily be reached by public transport: the train and bus 40 stop regularly. The map shows the buildings in the area, including Science Park 904. The observatory can not be reached by disabled visitors. Participating in the observing and the tours is therefore not possible.

De stargazing night is organized in cooperation with the amateur society Volkssterrenwacht Amsterdam.
